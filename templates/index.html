Mathematical Framework

The Gauss-Seidel method is an iterative technique we use to solve systems of linear equations in the form \( Ax = b \). It is closely related to the Jacobi method but generally achieves convergence with fewer iterations.

The primary distinction is its sequential updating strategy: as soon as we compute a new value for a variable, we immediately substitute it into the equations for the remaining variables within the exact same iteration loop.

Worked Example 1

Let's find the solution for the following linear system:

\( 4x_1 - x_2 = 3 \)
\( -x_1 + 4x_2 - x_3 = 5 \)
\( -x_2 + 4x_3 = 4 \)

First, we reorganize the equations to isolate each variable on the left side:

\( x_1 = \frac{3 + x_2}{4} \)

\( x_2 = \frac{5 + x_1 + x_3}{4} \)

\( x_3 = \frac{4 + x_2}{4} \)

We initiate the process with a zero vector guess: \( x_1=0, x_2=0, x_3=0 \).

Iteration 1:

  • \( x_1 = \frac{3 + 0}{4} = 0.75 \)
  • \( x_2 = \frac{5 + 0.75 + 0}{4} = 1.4375 \)
  • \( x_3 = \frac{4 + 1.4375}{4} = 1.359375 \)

Worked Example 2

Let's examine a second system of equations:

\( 5x_1 + x_2 + 2x_3 = 10 \)
\( x_1 + 4x_2 - x_3 = 5 \)
\( 2x_1 - x_2 + 5x_3 = 12 \)

We isolate our variables exactly as we did before:

\( x_1 = \frac{10 - x_2 - 2x_3}{5} \)

\( x_2 = \frac{5 - x_1 + x_3}{4} \)

\( x_3 = \frac{12 - 2x_1 + x_2}{5} \)

Using our initial guess of \( x_1=0, x_2=0, x_3=0 \).

Iteration 1:

  • \( x_1 = \frac{10 - 0 - 0}{5} = 2.0 \)
  • \( x_2 = \frac{5 - 2.0 + 0}{4} = 0.75 \)
  • \( x_3 = \frac{12 - 2(2.0) + 0.75}{5} = 1.75 \)
